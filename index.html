  • Phishing: Concept and How to Guard Against It

    Definition of Phishing Phishing is an attempt to deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or credit card details through fraudulent means like fake emails or deceptive text messages. Types of Phishing Traditional Phishing: Sending emails that appear to be from trusted sources, asking the recipient to click on a link or provide personal information. Spear Phishing: Targeting specific individuals or companies using personal information to make the attack appear more legitimate. Smishing: Sending deceptive text messages containing malicious links or requests for personal information. Vishing: Using phone calls to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information. How to Guard Against Phishing Verify the Source: Check the sender's email address and do not click on links in suspicious messages. Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security to your accounts. Training and Awareness: Be aware of phishing signs such as poor language and urgent requests. Use Security Software: Employ antivirus programs and firewalls. Report Phishing Attempts: Report any phishing emails to your email provider or the relevant organization..

    Conclusion Phishing poses a significant threat, but through awareness and proper precautions, its risks can be greatly reduced.
